## Barriers to Digital Transformation in SMEs within Complex Product Systems (CoPS)

SMEs operating in CoPS sectors (e.g., specialized aerospace components, niche shipbuilding systems, custom industrial machinery) face unique barriers due to the **inherent complexity of their products/systems** combined with **typical SME constraints**. Below is a structured analysis:

| Barrier                          | Explanation                                                                                                                                                               |
| :------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Resource Constraints**         | Limited capital restricts investment in foundational DT infrastructure (cloud, IoT sensors, AI platforms). Budgets prioritize immediate operational needs over long-term DT ROI. Lack of dedicated internal DT teams forces reliance on costly external consultants. |
| **Legacy System Integration**   | CoPS often rely on decades-old machinery, proprietary control systems, and siloed data formats. Integrating modern IoT/AI solutions with these heterogeneous, non-standardized legacy assets is technically complex, costly, and risks operational disruption. |
| **Specialized Skills Shortage** | Critical shortage of personnel skilled in *both* domain-specific CoPS engineering (e.g., fluid dynamics in turbines, structural acoustics in ships) *and* DT technologies (AI/ML, data engineering, cyber-physical systems). SMEs cannot compete with large firms on salaries for scarce hybrid talent. |
| **Data Fragmentation & Quality** | CoPS generate massive, multi-source data (sensors, CAD, supply chain, field logs) trapped in departmental/organizational silos. SMEs lack tools and governance frameworks to unify, clean, and contextualize this data – rendering AI/analytics ineffective due to poor input quality ("garbage in, garbage out"). |
| **Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities** | Increased connectivity (IoT, cloud) expands attack surfaces. SMEs often lack dedicated security teams, certified protocols, and incident response plans. Breaches risk catastrophic IP theft (e.g., proprietary designs), operational shutdowns, and loss of customer trust in safety-critical systems. |
| **Organizational Resistance**    | Deeply ingrained engineering cultures prioritize physical prototyping and empirical testing over digital simulation. Fear of job displacement, lack of DT literacy among veteran staff, and misalignment between engineering/production/management goals create passive or active resistance to new workflows. |
| **Supply Chain Digitization Gaps** | CoPS depend on intricate, multi-tier supplier networks. SMEs lack leverage to mandate digital standards (e.g., API integrations, shared digital twins) from smaller suppliers. Manual data exchange (emails, spreadsheets) between partners creates bottlenecks and errors in system integration. |
| **Uncertain ROI & Scaling Risk** | Long CoPS lifecycles (10-30+ years) delay DT payoff visibility. Project-based revenue models make sustained DT investment difficult. Pilot projects often fail to scale due to unanticipated complexity when moving from single components to full-system integration in unique customer configurations. |
| **Regulatory & Compliance Burden** | Highly regulated sectors (aerospace, rail, medical devices) require rigorous validation of *any* process change. SMEs struggle with the cost/time of recertifying digitally enhanced products or processes under frameworks like AS9100 (aerospace) or EN 5012x (railways), fearing liability if digital tools introduce undetected errors. |
| **Interoperability Deficits**    | Absence of universal standards for CoPS data exchange (e.g., between CAD tools, PLM systems, and field sensors). SMEs face costly customization to connect proprietary vendor platforms (Siemens vs. Dassault vs. PTC ecosystems), hindering end-to-end digital thread implementation. |

### Critical Context for SMEs in CoPS
*   **Complexity Multiplier:** Unlike standardized mass production, CoPS involve high customization, low-volume/high-variety production, and deep engineering integration. DT solutions must handle this variability, increasing implementation difficulty.
*   **Ecosystem Dependency:** Success hinges on digitizing interactions with customers, suppliers, and certification bodies – areas where SMEs have limited influence.
*   **Strategic Misalignment Risk:** DT initiatives often start as isolated "tech experiments" (e.g., adding sensors to one machine) without alignment to core business value drivers like reducing field failure rates or accelerating bespoke design cycles.

> **Academic/Practical Insight:** Research (e.g., Davies et al., *Research Policy*) shows CoPS innovation is inherently **project-based** and **system-integrative**. SMEs fail when they treat DT as an IT upgrade rather than a *socio-technical reconfiguration* of their entire value creation process. Case studies in offshore wind turbine SMEs reveal that overcoming these barriers requires starting with *modular* DT pilots targeting high-impact pain points (e.g., predictive maintenance for critical subsea components) while co-investing in supplier digitization partnerships.
