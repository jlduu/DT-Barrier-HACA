## The Strategic Framework for CoPS Digital Transformation

Digital transformation in CoPS isn't just about "going paperless"; it’s about transitioning from **document-centric** workflows to **data-centric** and **model-centric** ecosystems.

### 1. Core Pillar: Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE)

For CoPS, the "Digital Thread" must begin at the requirement stage.

* **The Goal:** Replace static PDFs and Excel sheets with dynamic models.
* **SME Advantage:** By using "Lean MBSE," SMEs can reduce costly physical prototyping and identify integration errors early in the design phase, where they are  to  cheaper to fix.

### 2. Integration of the Digital Twin

* **Design Twin:** Virtual simulation of system behavior.
* **Manufacturing Twin:** Optimizing the production logic of high-value, low-volume components.
* **Service Twin:** Using IoT sensors to monitor the health of the system in the field (e.g., a ship's propulsion system), enabling **Predictive Maintenance**.

### 3. Data-Driven Lifecycle Management (PLM)

SMEs often struggle with fragmented data. A unified **Product Lifecycle Management (PLM)** system acts as the "Single Source of Truth," ensuring that engineering, manufacturing, and maintenance teams are always working with the latest system configuration.
