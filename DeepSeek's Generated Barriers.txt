Of course. As an expert in the digital transformation (DT) of Complex Product Systems (CoPS), I have identified that SMEs face a unique set of challenges that are often more acute than those encountered by larger corporations. The high customization, systems integration intensity, and long lifecycles of CoPS create specific friction points when integrating digital technologies.

Here is a table outlining the key potential barriers for SMEs in CoPS during their digital transformation journey.

| Barriers | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Financial Constraints & Unclear ROI** | CoPS DT requires significant upfront investment in specialized software, hardware, sensors, and skilled personnel. For an SME, the capital outlay is substantial and the return on investment (ROI) is often long-term and difficult to quantify, especially for foundational upgrades like digital thread implementation or simulation platforms. This creates reluctance among stakeholders and makes securing funding challenging. |
| **2. Technical Complexity & Legacy Systems** | CoPS are often comprised of deeply integrated mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems. Retrofitting legacy assets with IoT sensors or connecting decades-old proprietary control systems to a modern data platform is technically arduous and expensive. The "brownfield" nature of their existing product base is a major hurdle compared to "greenfield" projects. |
| **3. Data Management & Interoperability** | DT generates vast amounts of heterogeneous data (e.g., from CAD, CAE, MES, ERP, and in-service sensors). SMEs often lack the data architecture and governance to manage this effectively. A critical barrier is achieving interoperability between disparate systems and ensuring data quality, consistency, and semantic meaning across the entire product lifecycle. |
| **4. Scarcity of Specialized Skills** | DT in CoPS requires a rare blend of skills: deep domain knowledge (e.g., aerodynamics, marine engineering) combined with digital expertise (e.g., data science, IoT, AI/ML). SMEs struggle to attract and retain such "bilingual" talent, who are in high demand and command high salaries from larger firms. |
| **5. Organizational Culture & Resistance** | The shift from a traditional, experience-driven engineering culture to a data-driven, agile, and collaborative one is profound. There can be significant resistance from engineers and managers who are skeptical of data-driven insights, fear job displacement, or are simply uncomfortable with new ways of working and increased transparency. |
| **6. Strategic Myopia & Lack of Vision** | Many SMEs embark on DT as a series of isolated technology projects (e.g., buying a new CAD system) without a cohesive strategic vision. Without a clear roadmap that aligns DT with business outcomes (e.g., shifting to product-service systems), efforts become fragmented, fail to deliver cumulative value, and ultimately lose momentum. |
| **7. Ecosystem Dependency & Supply Chain Pressure** | SMEs are typically part of a larger value chain, supplying to prime integrators (e.g., in aerospace or automotive). They face pressure to adopt specific digital standards and platforms dictated by their larger customers, often with little choice or support. This can lead to locked-in, costly solutions that don't necessarily benefit the SME's own operations. |
| **8. Cybersecurity & Intellectual Property Risks** | Connecting a highly valuable and sensitive CoPS to digital networks significantly expands the attack surface. SMEs may lack the resources and expertise to implement robust cybersecurity measures, making them vulnerable to attacks that could lead to theft of critical intellectual property, operational shutdown, or even safety-critical system compromises. |
| **9. Regulatory and Certification Hurdles** | CoPS industries (e.g., aerospace, medical devices, rail) are heavily regulated. Any digital change that affects the product's design, manufacturing process, or operational logic may require lengthy and costly re-certification processes. This creates a significant disincentive for innovation and rapid iteration. |

Understanding these barriers is the first step in developing a robust, phased, and pragmatic DT strategy tailored to the unique context of an SME in the CoPS domain. A successful transformation must address these points not just as technical challenges, but as interconnected strategic, organizational, and financial hurdles.
